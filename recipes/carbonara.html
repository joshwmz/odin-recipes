Carbonara

Description

A spaghetti carbonara recipe made the real traditional Italian way, relying sole on egg and chesse with no cream.

Ingredients

  • 175g guanciale (pancetta or block bacon)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 100g parmigiano reggiano
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 400g spaghetti
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1/2 cup pasta cooking water
  • 1 garlic clove

Steps

  1. Guanciale - Cut into 0.5cm thick slices then into batons.
  2. Carbonara sauce - Place eggs and yolks in a large bowl. Whisk to combine. Then stir in the parmesan and pepper.
  3. Cook pasta - Bring 4L of water to boil with the salt. Add pasta and cook per the packet directions.
  4. Reserve pasta water - Just before draining, scoop out 1 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta.
  5. Cook guanciale - While the pasta is cooking, place guanciale in a non stick pan over medium high heat. Cook for 4 to 5 minutes until golden. No oil needed - as the guanciale heats up, the fat will melt so it fries in its own fat. If using garlic, add it in the last minute.
  6. Pasta in pan - Tip the hot pasta into the pan and toss to coat in guanciale fat.
  7. Mix pasta in sauce - Transfer the pasta and any residual fat in the pan into the bowl with the egg. Add 1/2 cup pasta cooking water. Stir vigorously using the handle of a wooden spoon for 1 minute.
  8. Serve - Transfer into warm bowls, serve immediately. Garnish with a little extra parmigiana reggiano if desired, and a pinch of black pepper and finely chopped parsley.
